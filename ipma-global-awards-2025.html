What Is The IPMA Global Project Excellence Award?

The International Project Management Association (IPMA) is the world's oldest and most prestigious project management organisation, founded in 1965 and representing over 70 member nations across six continents.

The IPMA Global Project Excellence Awards recognise outstanding projects worldwide that demonstrate exceptional project management, stakeholder impact, innovation, and long-term social value. Projects compete through rigorous national and international evaluation panels comprising independent experts, academics, and industry leaders.

Receiving the Silver Award in the Social Project of the Year category confirms that FCI's Goodwood Social Housing Development is not only a national benchmark — it is a globally recognised model for affordable housing delivery, public-private partnership, and community upliftment.

Why This Project Won

Goodwood Station — Social Impact Story

The project that earned global recognition: a transit-oriented social housing development that redefines affordable living in South Africa.

🏠

Affordable Housing Delivery

Over 1,000 rental units delivered for households earning between R1,800 and R22,500/month — families otherwise excluded from formal housing markets.

🤝

Community Development

Beautifully landscaped secure courtyards, play areas for children, commercial retail spaces, and active community management create a thriving neighbourhood.

🏙️

Urban Regeneration

Transforming underutilised land around Goodwood railway station into a vibrant transit-oriented development — reversing spatial apartheid in Cape Town.

🤲

Public-Private Partnership

A landmark collaboration between FCI, PRASA, SHRA, and the City of Cape Town — showcasing how government and civil society can co-create housing at scale.

♻️

Sustainable Design

Energy-efficient heat-pump systems, solar supplementation, and eco-conscious construction methods reduce utility costs for residents and carbon emissions.

📈

Long-Term Social Impact

Reconnecting low-income families to schools, employment hubs, and rail corridors creates lasting socio-economic upliftment across generations.

From Vision To Reality

Goodwood Project Journey

Tracing the path of the Goodwood Social Housing Project from planning to international recognition.

December 2017

Stage 1: Planning & Development

FCI secures development rights around Goodwood Station in partnership with PRASA and the City of Cape Town. Architectural scoping begins for the landmark transit-oriented development.

January 2020

Stage 2: Construction Phase

Groundbreaking marks the commencement of construction. Framework build gets underway for the R575 million social housing project.

March 2024

Stage 3: Building Completion

Structural construction finishes, completing the 1,055-unit high-density complex near Goodwood railway station.

August 2024

Stage 4: Resident Occupation

Families move from informal settlements and shacks into secure, modern apartments at the new complex, gaining safe housing.

August 2024

Stage 5: Project Launch

The project is officially launched in an opening ceremony with Human Settlements Minister Mmamoloko Kubayi and Mayor Geordin Hill-Lewis.

October 2025

Stage 6: International Recognition

Goodwood receives international recognition at the IPMA Global Project Excellence Awards in Berlin, Germany, winning the Silver Award for Social Project of the Year.

April 2026

Stage 7: Reside Gold Award

Goodwood Station completes its historic double honors, earning South Africa's prestigious Reside Awards 2026 Gold Award for Social Housing Project of the Year.
